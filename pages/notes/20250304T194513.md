### Case Conversion

The [string-inflection](https://github.com/akicho8/string-inflection) project (see [this overview](http://sodaware.sdf.org/notes/converting-to-snake-case-in-emacs/)) converts symbol variables to _appropriate format_ for the mode. This replaces my home-brewed functions.

string-inflection

```emacs-lisp
(use-package string-inflection
  :general
  (:states '(normal visual motion operator)
           "z s" '("to snake case" . string-inflection-underscore)
           "z S" '("to Snake Case" . string-inflection-upcase)
           "z c" '("to camelCase" . string-inflection-lower-camelcase)
           "z C" '("to CamelCase" . string-inflection-camelcase)
           "z -" '("to kebab case" . string-inflection-kebab-case)
           "z z" '("toggle snake/camel" . string-inflection-all-cycle)))
```

### default 'g' bindings

I would like to have this bound on the `g` sequence, but that is crowded.

Note that `g u` (for lower-casing stuff), and `g U` (for up-casing) requires _something_, for instance `g U i o` upper-cases the symbol at point. These functions, however, only work with a symbol (which is the typical case).
